Scripps Grid Explorations

The following explorations highlight different approaches for the underlying architecture of the new Food.com redesign. Here, we explore two distinct adaptive layout techniques:

Fixed

The column & gutter widths remain constant with columns being either added or taken away based on available space. The benefit of this approach is ease of design & development. Since column widths do not change there are less opportunities for glitches to appear caused by wrapping and content that is too large.

Fluid

The entire site scales with the size of the browser. Designing for this method is essentially the same; at certain benchmarks all images and components would be at 100% and would scale down until the next benchmark is reached. Development is a bit more tricky in that percentages are used instead of pixels. The benefit of this type of grid is that the real estate is maximized for all resolutions and more 'future proof' in the event another device category using different resolutions enters the market.
